Our approach

What virtual support can include.

Virtual care can look different for every family. We focus on practical support that can be used at home, in daily routines, and in the moments that matter most.

◎

Virtual intake & planning

Start with a conversation about your concerns, goals, routines, and the kind of support you want for your child and family.

↗

Caregiver coaching

Learn practical strategies you can use during everyday routines, with support that fits into home life instead of adding more stress.

□

Focused therapy goals

Work on a few meaningful goals at a time, such as communication, behavior support, flexibility, routines, and daily living skills.

⌂

Home-based routines

Get support around transitions, play, independence, family routines, and other everyday moments where guidance can make a difference.

↺

Progress check-ins

Good fit

Is virtual care a good fit for your family?

Virtual support can be a strong option for many families, especially when caregiver involvement is a big part of the plan. It is also okay if you are not sure yet. We can talk that through together.

Virtual care may be a strong fit when:

  • ✓You want support that fits into home routines and everyday life.
  • ✓Your child can participate in short on-screen interactions, or a caregiver can support the session in person.
  • ✓Your goals include communication, routines, flexibility, behavior support, caregiver training, or daily living skills.
  • ✓You want easier access to care without the extra travel and scheduling burden.
  • ✓You are looking for focused support and clear next steps.

We may recommend hybrid or in-person support when:

  • !Your child needs hands-on support throughout the session.
  • !There are significant safety concerns that may be better addressed in person.
  • !Your family is looking for an intensive in-home or center-based model right away.
  • !You prefer to start with an in-person evaluation or a hybrid plan.
  • !You want help deciding whether virtual, hybrid, or in-person support makes the most sense.

Not always. Some families do very well with virtual caregiver coaching or focused therapy, while others may need hybrid or in-person support.
